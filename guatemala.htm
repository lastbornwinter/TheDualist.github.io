Guatemala: A forgotten culture

I travelled to Guatemala some years ago when I was much younger, and we all know how much more vibrant everything seems from the eyes of a child. We originally landed in Guatemala City. As well as being a beautiful city from the sky, it is the city with the 25th highest homicide rates in the world and is struggling to tackle a huge drug problem. It was these reasons combined with a few others that we left the city after one night there. We arrived back at the airport that we landed in after only a few hours on the ground and after a small admin technicality, we were back in the air. This time flying to the beautiful Lago de Izabal.

The lake itself was amazing and as we arrived at our hotel (called Finca Izabal) we felt fully immersed in our stunning surroundings. We stayed at Finca Izabal for a couple of nights, mainly relaxing and taking in a bit of Guatemalan culture. During our time there, we caught a boat down to the small coastal town of Livingstone. This was an immediate culture shock. We were no longer on the latin streets of a central american country, but on the streets of a small town in Jamaica. Due to it’s geographical location, Livingstone has a majority population of Caribbean people. You can buy plantains and eat local foods and even visit the waterfalls, surrounded by shrines to Bob Marley, and no, that’s not a joke.
